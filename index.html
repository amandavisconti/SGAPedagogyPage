SGA Pedagogy

Markup Pedagogy Pages for the Shelley-Godwin Archive

1. Zones <zone>

<zone type="main" "left_margin" "library" "pagination" "top">

The manuscript page is divided into zones. The "main" zone contains the main body of writing. The "pagination" zone contains page numbers supplied by the author. The "library" zone contains additional information, such as folio numbers, supplied by the holding library. The "top" zone contains chapter headings or titles. And the "left-margin" zone indicates that a margin has been created on the left side of the page using a vertical pencil line. For the Frankenstein manuscript, the left margin zone is used as a space for additions. The differing characteristics of manuscript sources will determine what kinds of zones will be used.

2. Lines <line>

Each line of writing in the manuscript is nested within the <line> element. 

<line>of the storm without wishing to</line>

3. Paragraphs <milestone unit="tei:p"/>

To indicate a paragraph break, use the <milestone> element. 

<line>This is the end of a paragraph.</line>
<milestone unit="tei:p"/><line><space extent="3"/>This is the start of a new paragraph.</line>

The backslash "/" indicates that the element is empty, so it does not require a closing element.

4. Simple Additions <add place="superlinear" "sublinear" "intralinear">

<line>the wild fancies of the<add place="intralinear" hand="pbs">se</add>
authors with</line>

Here, Percy has simply added "se" to the to make "these." For additions that appear above the line use the "superlinear" attribute, for those below use "sublinear".

<line>I <add place="superlinear" hand="#pbs">cheerfully</add> consented.</line>

5. Simple Deletions <del rend="strikethrough" "smear" "overwritten" "vertical_line">

<line>aplied myself to <del rend="strikethough">m</del> the more <del rend="strikethrough">ra</del> rational</line>

Here, the "m" and the "ra" have been struckthrough without any other modifications. The most common deletion type is a strikethrough. Less common forms of deletion include ink smears and overwrites. Because overwritten text includes both an addition and a deletion, it would be encoded within a <mod> element that links them together as a single authorial intervention. For example,

<line>th<mod><del rend="overwritten">is</del><add place="intralinear">e</add></mod> text has been overwritten</line>

The "is" in "this" has been written over with an "e" to make "the."

7. Simple Modifications <mod>

The <mod> element is used to group several revisions into one textual intervention: for example, substitutions, corrections, and overwrites. The <mod> element also allows us to encode the sequence of these changes. In the example below, "difference" was canceled and replaced by "alteration", which appears above the original line.

It is encoded as:

<line>a great <mod><del rend="strikethrough">difference</del><add place="superlinear">alteration</add></mod> 
in my sensations; before</line>

The <mod> element is also used to group together a caret and the additional text it signifies.

<line>. . . for <mod><add place="sublinear"><metamark>&#x2038;</metamark></add> 
<add place="superlinear">he</add></mod> was<del rend="strikethrough">con</del> constantly</line>

The caret was inserted below the line after "for" and indicates that the "he" added above the line should be inserted.

8. Cross-linear Modifications <mod><add next="">

Let's take a look at a complex modification that spans several lines. These are more difficult because of the nesting problems that arise when attempting to link together a modification that spans across the <line> element. Link cross-linear additions and deletions by inserting the "next" attribute within the element and linking it to an xml:id with the same value within the partner addition or deletion. 

<line><mod><del rend="strikethrough" next="#c56-0013.01">When it was most</del> 
	<add place="superlinear" hand="#pbs" next="#c56-0013.02>As I stood at the door</add></mod></line>
<line><mod><del rend="strikethrough xml:id="c56-0013.01">violent</del>, 
	<add place="superlinear" hand="#pbs" xml:id="c56-0013.02>on a sudden</add></mod></line>

We can now clearly see that "When it was most {line break} violent" was replaced with the superlinear addition of "As I stood at the door {line break} on a sudden"

9. Linking Material from Different Zones <ptr>

In the Frankenstein rough draft, many of the pages are divided by a vertical pencil line that creates an empty left margin within which to make additions or add comments. To link additions from the left margin to the text in the main draft zone, use the <ptr> (pointer) element. Text in the left margin is encoded as an addition, so nest it within the <add> or <addSpan> element. The xml:id is given within the <add> or <addSpan> element so that the <ptr target=""> element can identify within the main zone where the marginal addition is meant to be placed. Use <anchor/> to indicate the end of an <addSpan>. The <anchor/> element is empty and only indicates the end of a sequence of text. The xml:id in the <add> or <addSpan> element identifies the passage that is meant to be inserted into the main zone.

<zone type="main">
<line> . . . <add><ptr target="#c56-0014.02"/></add> . . . </line>
<zone/>

<zone type="left_margin">
	<addSpan xml:id="c56-0014.02" spanTo="#c56-0014.03" hand="#pbs"/>
	<line>its progress</line>
	<line>with curiosity</line>
	<line> &#x26; delight</line><anchor xml:id="c56-0014.03"/>
<zone/>

For a marginal addition of only a single line, simply place an xml:id within the <add> element to link it to the <ptr> element within the main zone.

<zone type="left_margin">
	<line><del rend="strikethrough"><add xml:id="#c57-0002.02" hand="#pbs">various</add></del></line>
</zone>

In the above example, the addition was later deleted, so it is nested within the <del> element.

10. Shifts in Hands <add hand="#pbs" "#comp"> <handShift new="#pbs">

To indicate an addition in a hand other than that of the primary author, use the "hand" attribute within the <add> element.

<line>string <mod><del rend="strikethrough">and</del><add place="superlinear" hand="#pbs>which</add></mod></line>

To indicate a shift in hands, use the <handShift> element. For example, in the fair-copy draft of Frankenstein, Percy takes over for Mary for an extended number of pages. Since this is not an addition, we mark the beginning of Percy's hand with <handShift new="#pbs/>. The backslash indicates that the element is empty and that the shift in hand continues until another shift in hand takes place.

12. Uncertain Readings <unclear unit="char" extent="">

Text that is illegible is enclosed within the <unclear> element. If the text is partially recognizable, indicate it as follows:

<line>During her confinement <unclear>mady</unclear> many</line>

If the text is obscured by ink blots, foxing, or is completely illegible. Use an empty <unclear> tag and indicate the approximate length of the uncertain reading. "char" stands for "characters".

<line>During her confinement <unclear unit="char" extent="4"/> many<line>

13. Encoding Spaces <space extent="">

To encode significant spaces between passages or words, use the <space> element and indicate the length of the space.

<line>acquired <space unit="char" extent="20"/> and</line>

Do not encode spaces unless there is a noticeable gap that extends well beyond the normal variation of spaces within a handwritten manuscript.

14. Damage to the Manuscript <damage>

If text is illegible or missing from manuscript because of damage, such as being torn or cut away, use the <damage> element. The reviewer will add more details to the encoding.

15. Paratextual Notations and Additions <note> <metamark>

For paratextual notations such as carets, brackets, and "X"s that indicate insertion points for added material, enclose them within the <metamark> element.

For notes or instructions added by either the author or compositor, use the <note> element. For example, in the fair-copy draft for Frankenstein, the compositor writes his initials and indicates where he either ends or begins typesetting: 

<line><note><add hand="#comp">F. W.<metamark>/</metamark>My courage . . .</line>
